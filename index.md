# Nick Holmes (born 1957)

Nick Holmes is a British hotelier and resort director whose career has spanned luxury hotels, golf and spa resorts, and hospitality consultancy across the UK. His life reflects a consistent thread of leadership, sport, music, and service, from his Wirral upbringing to senior roles in some of the country’s best-known resorts.

---

## Early life and family

Born in 1957, Nick Holmes was raised on the Wirral peninsula, living in Port Sunlight, New Ferry, and Thornton Hough.

He is the son of Donald Charles Holmes (1930–2024) and Mabel Patricia Holmes (1934–2022, known as Pat), and has one sister, Cheryl Murphy (née Holmes, born 1959).

Nick is married to Elizabeth Holmes (born 1962, Edinburgh), whom he met while working in Scotland. They have one daughter, Eleanor Mary Holmes (born October 1998).

---

## Education, sport, and early responsibility

Nick attended Wellington School, Bebington, before completing his secondary education at Wirral Grammar School, where he represented the school at rugby and was awarded school colours.

After leaving school, he worked at the Oval Sports Centre on the Wirral as a centre attendant, lifeguard, swimming instructor, and squash instructor. During this period, he qualified as an ASA Advanced Swimming Teacher.

During university holiday periods, he also worked as a lifeguard with the Wirral Beach Patrol, covering beaches across the peninsula, primarily based at West Kirby, including Hilbre Island.

Sport remained important into adulthood. He completed the Sheffield Marathon in 1982 (sub-four-hour time) and the Glasgow Marathon in 1986.

---

## Music: early life and later return

Music was a central interest during Nick Holmes’ teenage years. He played guitar in local bands on the Wirral and was a member of the rock group Swag, which reached the finals of a Wirral youth talent competition in the mid-1970s.

From the late 1970s, he was also active as a local DJ, playing events across the Wirral and occasionally Liverpool.

After a long professional career, he returned to formal music study and, in 2025, graduated from The Open University with a Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Music, achieving First-Class Honours.

---

## Hospitality education and training

Nick undertook a one-year general and practical catering course at St Helens Technical College, gaining City & Guilds qualifications in kitchen practice and food service.

He later studied at Sheffield Polytechnic (now Sheffield Hallam University), where he was awarded an HND in Hotel, Catering and Institutional Management. His training included work placements at The Lowood Hotel in the Lake District and Peebles Hydro in the Scottish Borders.

---

## Hospitality career

Nick Holmes built a career as a luxury hotelier, managing director, and resort developer, with particular expertise in hotel, golf, spa, and leisure operations.

### Scotland

- **Gleneagles Hotel (1982–1987):** Assistant Manager during a period of transition to year-round five-star operation.  
- **Craigendarroch Hotel, Royal Deeside:** General Manager, later Group Operations Director.  
- **Cameron House, Loch Lomond:** Played a key role in opening the resort in 1989 and served as Managing Director during its formative years.

### England

- **Branston Golf & Country Club:** Managing Director across two periods.  
- **Bristol Golf Club:** Opened the club and led its early development.

### Rockliffe Hall

From 2008 to 2014, Nick Holmes was Managing Director and General Manager of Rockliffe Hall, County Durham. He led the pre-opening and launch of the £70m luxury resort, achieving AA five-star status and national recognition, including VisitEngland Large Hotel of the Year. His appointment and leadership were widely covered in hospitality trade press and regional media.

### Consultancy and later roles

From 2014, he operated through NHC Management as a consultant and non-executive director, advising high-end hospitality projects including Ramside Hall, Swinton Park, Formby Hall, and Gisborough Hall.

In adult life, Nick Holmes lived in several parts of the UK, reflecting the national scope of his hospitality career. These included Edinburgh, Ballater (Royal Deeside), Burton upon Trent, Lichfield, Portishead, Darlington, and York.
